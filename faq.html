Frequently asked questions

The fund, the tax schemes and how investing works – in plain terms. Anything not covered here, email farid@doublecheque.com.

About the fund
What is DoubleCheque?+

DoubleCheque is a fund that helps back the next wave of British startups through SEIS and EIS. Every company is hand-picked and runs the full diligence gauntlet before a penny moves. Sapphire Capital Partners LLP, the fund's FCA-authorised manager, takes every final investment decision.

What does the fund invest in?+

UK early-stage companies, on a generalist basis – the fund is not tied to a single sector. Each cohort targets a portfolio of around 12 holdings.

What makes DoubleCheque different?+

Every company clears the same double-check. More than half are existing clients of our sister accountancy practice, so their numbers have already been examined by an accountant. On top of that, each company must hold HMRC SEIS/EIS advance assurance before any money moves.

Who makes the investment decisions?+

Double Cheque sources each opportunity and runs the diligence. Sapphire Capital Partners, the fund's FCA-authorised manager, takes every final decision. Because the same team advises the fund and runs the practice that supplies much of the deal flow, this is a related-party arrangement – disclosed in full in the Information Memorandum.

SEIS & EIS
What are SEIS and EIS?+

They are two UK government schemes designed to encourage investment into early-stage companies. In return for the risk of backing young businesses, investors can claim generous tax reliefs – up to 50% income tax relief under SEIS and 30% under EIS – plus further reliefs on gains and losses.

What tax reliefs could I receive?+

Depending on your circumstances, SEIS and EIS can offer up-front income tax relief (up to 50% SEIS / 30% EIS), tax-free growth if shares are held for at least three years, capital gains reliefs, and loss relief if a company fails.

Reliefs depend on your individual circumstances and on the companies keeping their qualifying status, and the rules can change. This is not tax advice – please take your own.

When do I need to invest to claim relief for a tax year?+

The fund is evergreen, so you can apply at any time. Invest before 6 April 2027 to set reliefs against the 2025/26 or 2026/27 tax years. Capital is at risk.

Is my capital at risk?+

Yes. This is a high-risk, long-term investment. Investing in early-stage companies puts your capital at risk and you may lose all of it. These investments are illiquid and hard to sell. Only invest money you can afford to lose.

Investing
Who can invest?+

The fund is open only to investors who qualify as high-net-worth or sophisticated. You will be asked to confirm your classification as part of applying.

What is the minimum investment?+

The minimum subscription is £25,000.

What return does the fund target?+

The fund targets a return of 3× over 5 years. This is gross of fees and before any SEIS/EIS tax reliefs; net returns will be lower. It is a target, not a forecast or a guarantee of future returns – capital is at risk and you may get back less than you invest.

What are the fees?+

The fund's charges are set out in full in the Information Memorandum and Key Information Document. Request the documents and we'll send them to you.

How do I get the Information Memorandum & KID?+

Leave your email under Download the documents on the home page and we'll send you the Information Memorandum and Key Information Document. No call, no commitment.

How do I apply to invest?+

Use Apply to invest to register your interest. We'll send the Information Memorandum and arrange a call. No money is committed at that stage.

For founders
My company is raising – can DoubleCheque invest?+

We back UK companies that hold SEIS or EIS advance assurance, or are eligible for it. Send us your company through For founders – we read every submission and reply within ten working days.

Do you help fill the rest of the round?+

Yes. Alongside investing, we can make introductions to our investor network, handle SEIS/EIS advance assurance and paperwork, and co-ordinate the raise through to close. The success fee is paid only when the round closes.
